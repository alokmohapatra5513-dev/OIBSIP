About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was one of India's greatest scientists, teachers, and visionaries. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble family but achieved extraordinary success through hard work, dedication, and education.

He played a major role in India's space and missile development programs. Because of his contributions to missile technology and national defense, he became popularly known as the "Missile Man of India."

In 2002, he became the 11th President of India. Unlike many political leaders, he remained deeply connected with students and inspired millions to dream big, work hard, and contribute to the nation.

Even after completing his presidency, he continued teaching and motivating young minds until his last breath in 2015. His life remains a symbol of humility, innovation, leadership, and service to humanity.

Major Achievements

1931

1960

1998

Played a vital role in the Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests.

2002

Became the 11th President of India.

2015

Passed away while delivering a lecture to students.
